City of Mesquite

GIS Coordinator

October 2016 - Present

  • Develop and manage the City's Unmanned Aerial System(UAS) program, which has provided a cost-effective solution for acquiring high-resolution GIS data, such as centimeter level Digital Elevation Models and orthomosaic imagery, resulting in cost savings on engineering projects with budgetary constraints and facilitating policy-making for department heads and city council.
  • Streamlined communications between employees and management through the implementation and use of Kanban, and other Agile project management methodologies.
  • Develop and maintain GIS services to help departments achieve their goals, such as creating a custom web editor using ArcGIS API for JavaScript 3.23 to assist the Police Department deploy, track, and analyze the efficacy of crime prevention operations.
  • Developed custom geoprocessing tools using the ArcPy Python library to automate GIS tasks, such as creating an ArcMap Geoprocessing Tool to assist with engineering as-built record requests. When benchmarked against a request that previously took a day's work, this script was able to provide a 30-minute turnaround, and identified previously missing records.

City of Mesquite

GIS Technician

April 2016 - October 2016

  • Developed metrics using Python and R to assist the City in data-driven decision making, such as performing a multi-criteria analysis to redistrict ECODE inspector boundaries based on a projected caseload score, with the objective of addressing workload imbalance. To-date, the resulting boundaries have been in effect for 16 months with positive feedback from all inspectors.
  • Used skills in GIS and T-SQL to facilitate departmental projects, such as assisting the finance department in developing a methodology for calculating Drainage Utility District (DUD) fees, which has created significant additional revenue for maintenance and capital improvements.
  • Introduced new GIS technologies and analytical methodologies aimed to increase productivity and decrease City expenditures, such as promoting the use of UAS remote sensing technologies to supplement surveys and reduce the cost of GIS data acquisition.
